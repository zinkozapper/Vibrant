A Comparison of Joan of Arc & The Book of Mormon

The Maid of Orléans

Joan of Arc (c. 1412–1431) was a simple peasant girl whose conviction altered the course of history. Guided by celestial voices, she donned armor and led the French army to miraculous victories during the Hundred Years' War.

A figure of immense faith and courage, her life offers a striking parallel to the prophetic callings found within The Book of Mormon—both speaking of divine appointment, the burden of leadership, and the trial of faith.
